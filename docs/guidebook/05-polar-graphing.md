# Chapter 5: Polar Graphing

Polar graphing plots curves given as a radius in terms of an angle, the
natural form for spirals, cardioids, and rose curves. When this mode
arrives it will sit on the same graph engine as Chapter 4: Cartesian
Graphing, Drawing, Formats, and Persistence: a `Pol` graph mode, polar
equation slots edited the same way as `Y1` through `Y3`, plotting with the
same cancellable column-by-column redraw, tracing and tables driven by the
angle instead of `X`, the analyses of chapter 4, and a `PolarGC` setting to
read graph coordinates as a radius and angle. The angle mode on the mode
screen (Chapter 1: Operating the Calculator) will decide whether the angle
is stepped in radians or degrees.

None of this exists in today's firmware.

> ⚠ **Planned:** the polar graphing workflow: the `Pol` mode, the polar
> equation editor, plotting, tracing, tables, analysis, and the `PolarGC`
> coordinate display (Free85 2.0, work package 14.5).

What the keys do today: [GRAPH] always opens the Cartesian graph screen of
chapter 4 and plots the function slots in `X`; there is no graph-type
setting on the mode screen, which offers only angle, display format, and
contrast; and the catalog (chapter 1) has no polar entries. Until the mode
exists, single polar points can still be worked out on the home screen with
the trigonometry of Chapter 3: Mathematics, Calculus, and Comparisons: a
point at radius 2 and angle `PI/6` has
`2*COS(PI/6)` and `2*SIN(PI/6)` as its Cartesian coordinates, answering
`= 1.7320508075689` and `= 1`.
